  • Fujairah Whale and Dolphin Project

    Marine mammal observer and leader of the 1st dedicated cetaceans project in the Emirate of Fujairah which aims to Identification and long-term continuous monitoring of whales, dolphins, and other marine mammals and study of the behavior, space, and habitat used by cetaceans for resting and feeding. 

    See Project

  • Artificial Reef Project

    As the project leader, I spearheaded the development of artificial reefs using oyster shells and palm leaves to support coral rehabilitation and biodiversity conservation. The initiative involved conducting an Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA), designing the reefs, and overseeing their construction and implementation. Additionally, the project aimed to evaluate which reef design most effectively enhances fish stocks.

    See Project

  • water quality monitoring

    Establish a continuous monitoring program for the reef habitat to assess the impact of human activities and identify water quality by collecting water samples and doing the lab analysis. Aiming to study how the levels of heavy metals, microbial analysis, pH, and salinity vary across different marine habitats and investigate the habitats resilient to water quality changes

    See Project

  • Marine Habitat Map

    Identify and classify different marine habitats within Fujairah marine waters, such as coral reefs, seagrass beds, and sandy bottoms, and Assess the health condition of each habitat to inform conservation and management efforts. Through collaboration with the diving centers, private companies, and environmental agencies to provide us with the existing data. Moreover, using satellite imagery and doing field surveys.
